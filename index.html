China VS Taiwan

Using R to analyze how China and Taiwan are mentioned
on Twitter, The Now York Times and Facebook

About China and Taiwan

The relationship between China and Taiwan is an eternal and complicated topic.

For us Chinese, we always strongly believe that Taiwan being a part of China is an irrefutable fact. But in the world-wide range, it is totally not that case. Many countries are confused about their relationship, and some even deliberately use the conflict between the two regions to restrict China’s further development.

So, I want to know more about how the international community talk about or comment on China and Taiwan.

Twitter Wordcloud

Firstly, I made a wordcloud for “China” and “Taiwan”, seeing the words that appear with them most frequently on Twitter. I respectively selected the latest 1000 tweets that mentioned “China” and “Taiwan” and analyzed the whole text through R Studio.

It is shown that for “Taiwan”, words with the highest frequency are all related to the earthquake, like Hualien, Taiwan earthquake, pray for Taiwan, rescue, survivors and so on. We can see that the earthquake is what the international community concern most about Taiwan in recent days.

For China, it is interesting to see the word “ecommerce” and “internet business”. We all know that China’s ecommerce is at the world’s leading position. The two biggest online shopping markets Taobao and JD provide people with multiple commodity choices and efficient delivery service. And those leading Internet companies Alibaba, Tencent, Baidu are now performing increasingly better on the worlds stage.

The appearance of “hongkong”, I guess is due to the recent BU student protesting Mandarin test event. And in political aspect, China is also mentioned together with India, North Korea, Japan, USA and Russia.

NYT and Facebook

I also figure out the frequency of the two key words on NYT during the time period of 1987 to 2017. With the regard of China, there is a dramatic growth between 2004 and 2008. Personally I think the reason may be 2008 Beijing Olympic Games. After the Athens Olympic Games, the world started to focus on and look forward to China’s performance. The 2008 Olympic Games strongly increased China’s sense of presence in the international community and boosted its tourism economy.

There is a small rise for Taiwan in 2016. Maybe it is because of the Taiwan Election. Overall, China was mentioned much more than Taiwan on the New York Times.

For Facebook, I try to analyze the occurrence of “China” and “Taiwan” on four mainstream media’s Facebook pages, saying CNN, BBC, POLITICO, ABC. Because I met the situation that some media didn’t mention “China” or “Taiwan” in its latest 100 posts, I created a function to select their latest 500 posts.

It can be seen that China was mentioned more than Taiwan by BBC and POLITICO. For CNN, both of the words were mentioned 3 times. And ABC mentioned Taiwan more than China.
